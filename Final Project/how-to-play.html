How to Play Truco

Truco is played with a Spanish deck of 40 cards (no 8s or 9s). Each player is dealt 3 cards. The objective is to win two out of three rounds in a hand using the hierarchy of cards and special declarations like "Truco," "Envido," and "Flor."

Basic Rules

  • Each round, players play one card at a time.
  • The highest card wins the round. Best two out of three wins the hand.
  • Players can call "Truco" to raise the stakes — the opponent can accept or fold.

Card Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest)

  1. Ancho de Espadas (1 of swords)
  2. Ancho de Bastos (1 of clubs)
  3. 7 of swords
  4. 7 of gold
  5. 3s, 2s, 1 of cups and coins
  6. 12s, 11s, 10s, 7 of cups/clubs, 6s, 5s, 4s

Special Calls

  • Envido: Declare to compare point values of two cards of the same suit.
  • Truco: A bluffing challenge to win more points — can be escalated to "Retruco" and "Vale Cuatro."
  • Flor: A rare call if you have all three cards of the same suit — beats everything.

Truco is as much about bluffing and reading your opponent as it is about card values. Learn the signals, have fun, and don’t be afraid to shout "¡Truco!"
